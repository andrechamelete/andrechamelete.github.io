About us

There is no denying that the world is more complex than ever. Technologies such as artificial intelligence, blockchain and automation have transformed all sectors, altering the way companies operate and people interact. This complexity demands constant adaptation, fast and efficient decision-making, and measures that challenge known planning and management models.


The Beginning


Our company was born out of dissatisfaction with how agility has been treated by most organizations and, especially, by professionals in the field.


Agile methodologies emerged as a response to the traditional management model, known as Waterfall. However, they ended up becoming just pre-fabricated models that do not take into account the context, challenges and specific needs of each company. In other words, these are formats that foster resistance to change more than results, and survive on the promise of serving any organization. And yes, they even work for some. However, according to studies by PMI and McKinsey, 70% of agile transformations (i.e., implementations of such frameworks) end in a state where the results are the same, or even worse, than those of the previous context.


We see and treat agility differently!


What is Agility


The concept comes from the world of sports. Think, for example, of a soccer player: An agile athlete is one who can react to opponents and the game situation without losing speed or, at least, regaining it quickly. This is the idea that gives rise to our approach. We treat agility not as the name for a set of rigid rules that cannot be waived, but rather as a skill to be developed, just like in sports.


We seek to understand all the methodologies, models and tools. However, we do not limit ourselves to any one of them. We use from each one what works for our client, and discard everything else. We do not accept the rules imposed by agile frameworks. Instead, we understand the company and its challenges to create customized solutions that will truly bring value.


We also avoid disruptive implementations, major transformations that cause more resistance and perplexity than results. We approach changes in an evolutionary manner, through small improvements only where necessary, so that they are naturally incorporated into the organization's processes and culture, without generating stress or obstacles.


In short, what sets our approach to these "agile transformations" apart is that we do not try to restructure companies to fit into external models, as if there were nothing good to be gained. The opposite is true: we shape solutions that fit your organization, keeping what is good and gradually improving what can be improved, so that the capacity to be agile is developed, thus minimizing resistance to change and its consequent stress.


Outcomes


Organizational agility: the ability to adapt to market volatility and respond to customer needs while maintaining a sustainable pace that fosters growth.


Cost reduction: healthy workflows reduce waste, improving the profit margin of the operation, which enables the growth of your company.


Continuous improvement: development of a culture with an evolutionary stance, of always learning and proactively seeking progress in situations.


Optimized decision-making: development of managers and leaders capable of leveraging the expertise of their teams while still having control of the situation.


Elimination of work overload: non-overworked employees generate better solutions without the stress of demands, improving the quality of life at work and making it easier to retain talent.


Transparency: with clearer processes and rules and effective visualization, management can monitor teams more assertively, with fewer meetings and reports, enabling a more agile response to challenges.
